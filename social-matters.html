social matters

An influencer marketing platform for influencer identification and campaign reporting

BRIEF

Design an owned influencer identifiaction and campaign reporting tool which has the potential to be rolled out on a subscription basis

CLIENT

Matter of Form x Social Matters

ROLE

UX Research, UI/UX design, wireframing

TIMELINE

10 weeks

Stakeholder interview

The discovery phase began with a stakeholder interview to gain a deeper understanding of influencer marketing as an industry, what Social Matters offers as a business, and how they invision the tool.
Here’s what I learnt:

Social Matters, offers influencer marketing services to luxury brands across the fashion, beauty and luxury lifestyle industries. Their full service includes:

Creative strategy | Influencer identification | Project management | Campaign reporting

The purpose of the tool is to facilitate Influencer identification and campaign reporting processes.

MOTIVATIONS

Social Matters currently use multiple platforms to identfiy influencers and manually track campaigns.
This is both time consuming and costly.

GOALS

Short term:
An owned service for influencer identification and campaign reporting.
Long term:
A managed and subscription service

USERS

Short term:
Primary users: Internal team
Long term:
Primary users: Internal team.Secondary users: External agencies or brands

Surveys & interviews | Insights

15Survey participants
4User interviews
The next step was to understand current users’ experiences, usage and pain points with existing platforms.
First I created a short survey, and shortlisted a range of users to interview.
Here are some common themes that cropped up amongst primary and secondary users.
TIME & COST
Multiple tools are costly, and are not time efficient to use.
BUDGET
Agencies lose profit margin due to influencer price vs. client budget. Client budget does not always cover the end to end process
FILTERS
Filters are extremely important as they save time and hone searches. Filters must be relevant and easy to use.
PROFILE STATISTICS
The following are the most important when identifying influencers:
- Engagement rate
- Reach
- Audience demographics

Competitors | Research & analysis

Completing a competitor analysis was useful to understand competitor features, overall aesthetic, and to see where Social Matters stands out.

During the intitial stakeholder interview, we discussed features for the software taking into consideration what the primary user felt would be most useful to a luxury agency. It was interesting to se that no competitor had a luxury affiinity measure, the ability to generate look a like profiles nor an option for a managed vs subscription service, further reaffirming Social Matters unique value proposition.

I identified “Traackr” as an overall star player as it had the best filtering options and system, look and feel, and features overall.

Personas | Primary user

Based on the research gathered during the discovery phase, I created 2 personas that represent the primary and secondary users.

Hannah Penn

Head of Social Media & Influencer marketing

London, UK

BEHAVIOUR:

  • -She uses multiple software to identify influencers.
  • -Usage of platforms varies from month to month.
  • -Manually tracks Campaign metrics
  • -Instagram is the most relevant platform today.

FRUSTRATIONS/PAIN POINTS:

  • -Time consuming to trawl through data on multiple platforms, and to manually track and collate campaign reporting metrics.
  • -Costly to have multiple software subscriptions.
  • -Client cannot always afford a complete end-to-end service, as budget is taken up by other costs.

GOALS:

  • -Identify influencers faster and more easily.
  • -Spend more time on creative strategy and project management.
  • -To have one software/subscription for influencer identification & reporting, that collates all campaign reports automatically.
  • -Able to offer smaller budget clients a part managed service.

Personas | Secondary user

Alice Stone

Associate director

London, UK

BEHAVIOUR:

  • -Uses multiple software to identify influencers.
  • -Uses specific software for specific platforms.
  • -Team mate works on campaign reporting
  • -Puts forward YouTubers mostly, as they’re easier to find.

FRUSTRATIONS/PAIN POINTS:

  • A lot of time wasted going between software.
  • Would like to work with instagram influencers more but struggles to find them.
  • “Sometimes we don’t make much profit, because client budgets is used up by the cost of influencers”
  • Lack of transparency and frequent communication can be difficult, which has a negative effect on campaigns.
  • Unaware of new software available.

GOALS:

  • To use one software that indexes influencers across all platforms, that is affordably priced.
  • The ability to find influencers faster.
  • To have a software that multiple users can use at the same time, that identifies influencers and manages campaign reporting.
  • To be more aware of software and influencer marketing trends.

Experience map | Identifying an influencer

I created the experience map below to understand the current tasks involved in identifying an influencer. The grey areas highlight where the user’s experience starts to dip- creating opportunity areas to optimise the overall experience. Tasks such as searching, screening, shortlisting, and data inputting are what the user finds time consuming and most frustrating.

Problem statement

Below is the problem statement I plan to focus on based on the user’s painpoints, usuage and stakeholder requirements.

“Social Matters needs a service that offers a multitude of filters and profile statistics because it allows users to identify influencers faster, and saves them using multiple software to complete one task. “
